# Pralent Program Rules & Code of Conduct

## 1\. BINDING AGREEMENT.

To enter the Program, you must agree to the Pralent Program Rules & Code of Conduct, which incorporate by reference the provisions and content of the Program Website and any Specific Program Rules above (collectively, the "Rules"). Please read these Rules carefully before entry to ensure you understand and agree. You further agree that submission of an entry in the Program constitutes agreement to these Rules. You may not submit an entry to the Program and are not eligible to receive the awards associated with this Program ("Awards") unless you agree to these Rules. These Rules form a binding legal agreement between you and the Program Sponsor with respect to the Program.

## 2\. ELIGIBILITY. 

A. To be eligible to enter the Program, you must be:

1.  Registered at pralent.com

2.  You affirm that you are at least the age of majority where you reside, or an emancipated minor, or possess legal parental or guardian consent, and are fully able and competent to enter into the terms, conditions, obligations, affirmations, representations, and warranties set forth in this Agreement. You also affirm that you are able to abide by and comply with this Agreement.

3.  pralent.com is not intended for children under thirteen. You affirm that you are at least thirteen years of age. If you are under thirteen years of age, then please do not use pralent.com. (Although Pralent, Inc. does not knowingly accept registrations from users under the age of thirteen, minors can still enter and win Programs by having their parents or guardians register for Pralent on their behalf.)

4.  Not a resident of Crimea, Cuba, Iran, Syria, North Korea, or Sudan

5.  Not a person or representative of an entity under U.S. export controls or sanctions (see <https://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/sanctions/Programs/Pages/Programs.aspx>).

6.  Other local rules and regulations may apply to you, so please check your local laws to ensure that you are eligible to participate in skills-based Programs. The Program Sponsor reserves the right to award alternative Awards where needed to comply with local laws.

If you are entering as a representative of a company, educational institution or other legal entity, or on behalf of your employer, these rules are binding on you, individually, and the entity you represent or are an employee. If you are acting within the scope of your employment, as an employee, contractor, or agent of another party, you warrant that such party has full knowledge of your actions and has consented thereto, including your potential receipt of a Award. You further warrant that your actions do not violate your employer's or entity's policies and procedures.

The Program Sponsor reserves the right to verify eligibility and to adjudicate on any dispute at any time. If you provide any false information relating to the Program concerning your identity, residency, mailing address, telephone number, email address, ownership of right, or information required for entering the Program, you may be immediately disqualified from the Program.

B. Unless otherwise stated in the Specific Program Rules above or prohibited by internal policies of the Program Entities, employees, interns, contractors, officers and directors of Program Entities may enter and participate in the Program, but are not eligible to win any Awards. "Program Entities" means the Program Sponsor, Pralent Inc., and their respective parent companies, subsidiaries and affiliates. If you are such a participant from a Program Entity, you are subject to all applicable internal policies of your employer with respect to your participation.

## 3\. SPONSOR AND HOSTING PLATFORM.

The Program is sponsored by Program Sponsor named in the Specific Program Rules. The Program is hosted on behalf of Program Sponsor by Pralent Inc. ("Pralent"). Pralent is an independent contractor of Program Sponsor, and is not a party to this or any agreement between you and Program Sponsor. You understand that Pralent has no responsibility with respect to selecting the potential Program winner(s) or awarding any Awards. Pralent will perform certain administrative functions relating to hosting the Program, and you agree to abide by the provisions relating to Pralent under these Rules. As a pralent.com account holder and user of the Pralent Program platform, remember you have accepted and are subject to the Pralent Terms of Service at [www.pralent.com/terms](http://www.pralent.com/terms) in addition to these Rules.

## 4\. Program PERIOD.

For the purposes of Awards, the Program will run from the Start Date and time to the End Date and time (such duration the "Program Period"). The Program Timeline is subject to change, and Program Sponsor may introduce additional hurdle deadlines during the Program Period. Any updated or additional deadlines will be publicized on the Program Website. It is your responsibility to check the Program Website regularly to stay informed of any deadline changes. You are responsible for determining the corresponding time zone in your location.

## 5\. Program ENTRY. 

NO PURCHASE NECESSARY TO ENTER OR WIN. To enter the Program, you must register on the Program Website prior to the Registration Date and time or End Date and time if there is no Registration Date and time, and follow the instructions for developing and entering your Submission through the Program Website. Your Submissions must be made in the manner and format, and in compliance with all other requirements, stated on the Program Website (the "Requirements"). Submissions must be received before any Submission deadlines stated on the Program Website. Submissions not received by the stated deadlines will not be eligible to receive a Award.

Submissions are void if they are in whole or part illegible, incomplete, damaged, altered, counterfeit, obtained through fraud, or late. Program Sponsor or pralent.com reserves the right to disqualify any entrant who does not follow these Rules, including making a Submission that does not meet the Requirements.

## 6\. INDIVIDUALS AND TEAMS.

A. Individual Account. You may make Submissions only under one, unique Pralent.com account. You will be disqualified if you make Submissions through more than one Pralent account, or attempt to falsify an account to act as your proxy.

B. Teams. If permitted under the Program Website guidelines, multiple individuals may collaborate as a team (a "Team"); however, you may join or form only one Team. Each Team member must be a single individual with a separate Pralent account. If the Program requires it, ALL Team Members must register to the Program before the Registration Deadlines Outlined above. Team Members will be added to Projects which will then be submitted to a Program. You must confirm your Team membership to make it official by responding to the Team notification message sent to your Pralent account. Team membership for a Project may not exceed the Maximum Team Size stated on the Program Website.

7\. Program DATA and API.

"Program Data" or "Program API" means the data or datasets or apis available from the Program Website for the purpose of use in the Program, including any prototype or executable code provided on the Program Website.

A. Data and API Access and Use. You may access and use the Program Data or Program API for non-commercial purposes only, including for participating in the Program, and for academic research and education. The Program Sponsor reserves the right to disqualify any participant who uses the Program Data or Program API other than as permitted by the Program Website and these Rules.

B. Data and API Security. You agree to use reasonable and suitable measures to prevent persons who have not formally agreed to these Rules from gaining access to the Program Data or Program API. You agree not to transmit, duplicate, publish, redistribute or otherwise provide or make available the Program Data or Program API to any party not participating in the Program. You agree to notify Pralent immediately upon learning of any possible unauthorized transmission of or unauthorized access to the Program Data or Program API and agree to work with Pralent to rectify any unauthorized transmission or access.

8\. SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS.

A. Private Information Sharing. Unless otherwise specifically permitted under the Program Website or Program Specific Rules above, during the Program Period, Privately sharing code, data, information outside of your team is not permitted. [Plagiarism](https://www.plagiarism.org/article/what-is-plagiarism) of your project, code, written work, images, video, publications, designs, ideas, etc. is prohibited and will result in disqualification from the program, future programs, and past programs, as well as account termination of all members involved in the submission and project.

B. Use of Open Source. Unless otherwise stated in the Specific Program Rules above, if open source code or information is used to generate the Project and/or Submission, then you must only use open source code or information licensed under an Open Source Initiative-approved license (see [www.opensource.org](http://www.opensource.org/)) that in no event limits commercial use of such code or information containing or depending on such code or information.

## 9\. DETERMINING WINNERS. 

Each Submission will be scored and ranked based on the information stated on the Program Website. The potential winner(s) are determined by the Program Sponsor, subject to compliance with these Rules. In the event of a tie, the Submission that was entered first to the Program will be the winner. In the event a potential winner is disqualified for any reason, the Submission that is placed next as determined by the Program Sponsor will be chosen as the potential winner.

## 10\. NOTIFICATION OF WINNERS & DISQUALIFICATION.

The potential winner(s) will be notified by email.

If a potential winner (i) does not respond to the notification attempt within one (1) week from the first notification attempt or (ii) notifies Pralent within one week after the End Date that the potential winner does not want to be nominated as a winner or does not want to receive a Award, then, in each case (i) and (ii) such potential winner will not receive any Award, and an alternate potential winner will be selected from among all eligible entries received based on the Program's reviewing criteria.

In case (i) and (ii) above Pralent may disqualify the participant. However, in case (ii) above, if requested by Pralent, such potential winner may provide their Project, Submission, and documentation to verify the participant's compliance with these Rules. If the potential winner provides their Project, Submission, and documentation to the satisfaction of Pralent, the participant will not be disqualified pursuant to this paragraph.

Program Sponsor reserves the right to disqualify any participant from the Program if the Program Sponsor reasonably believes that the participant has attempted to undermine the legitimate operation of the Program by cheating, deception, or other unfair playing practices or abuses, threatens or harasses any other participants, Program Sponsor or Pralent.

Disqualification will result in removal from the program, future programs, and past programs, as well as account termination of all members involved in the disqualification acts from the submission and project.

## 11\. WINNERS OBLIGATIONS.

As a condition to being awarded a Award, a Award winner must fulfill the following obligations:

(a) Team and Team members have and shall retain sole and exclusive title and ownership of all Software and Submissions, and all Intellectual Property Rights relating thereto, unless: (i) the Program Rules state clearly that the Team and Team members will be required to transfer property of the Submission and all Intellectual Property Rights relating thereto to the Program Sponsor as a requirement of the Program; AND (ii) an award is paid or made by the Program Sponsor to such Team and Team members.

(b) abide by any other obligations as stated in the Program Specific Rules on the Program Website by the Program Sponsor.

(c) sign and return all Award acceptance documents as may be required by Program Sponsor or Pralent, including without limitation: (i) eligibility certifications; (ii) licenses, releases and other agreements required under the Rules; and (iii) U.S. tax forms (such as IRS Form W-9 if U.S. resident, IRS Form W-8BEN if foreign resident, or future equivalents).

## 12\. AWARDS. 

Award(s) are as described on the Program Website and are only available for winning during the time period described on the Program Website. The odds of winning any Award depends on eligible Submission(s) received during the Program Period and the skill of the participants.

All Awards are subject to Program Sponsor's review and verification of the participant's eligibility and compliance with these Rules, and the compliance of the winning Submissions with the Submissions Requirements. In the event that the Submission demonstrates non-compliance with these Program Rules, Program Sponsor may at its discretion take either of the following actions: (i) disqualify the Submission(s); or (ii) require the potential winner to remediate within one week after notice all issues identified in the Submission(s) (including, without limitation, the resolution of license conflicts, the fulfillment of all obligations required by software licenses, and the removal of any software that violates the software restrictions).

A potential winner may decline to be nominated as a Program winner in accordance with Section 10.

Potential winners must return all required Award acceptance documents within two (2) weeks following notification of such required documents, or such potential winner will be deemed to have forfeited the award and another potential winner will be selected. Award(s) will be awarded within approximately 30 days after receipt by Program Sponsor or Pralent of the required Award acceptance documents. Transfer or assignment of a Award is not allowed.

You are not eligible to receive any Award if you do not meet the Eligibility requirements in Section 2 above.

If a Team wins a monetary Award, the Award money will be allocated in even shares between the eligible Team members, unless the Team unanimously opts for a different Award split and notifies Pralent before Awards are issued.

## 13\. TAXES.

ALL TAXES IMPOSED ON AWARDS ARE THE SOLE RESPONSIBILITY OF THE WINNERS. Payments to potential winners are subject to the express requirement that they submit all documentation requested by Program Sponsor or Pralent for compliance with applicable state, federal, local and foreign (including provincial) tax reporting and withholding requirements. Awards will be net of any taxes that Program Sponsor is required by law to withhold. If a potential winner fails to provide any required documentation or comply with applicable laws, the Award may be forfeited and Program Sponsor may select an alternative potential winner. Any winners who are U.S. residents will receive an IRS Form-1099 in the amount of their Award.
